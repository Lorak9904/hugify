Suggestions for Improvement

If you feel that your mood is fluctuating a lot, here are some steps you can take:

  • Breathing Exercises: Try some deep breathing exercises to calm your nerves. Breathe in for 4 seconds, hold for 7 seconds, and exhale for 8 seconds.
  • Daily Journaling: Writing down your thoughts and feelings can be helpful to process emotions and improve mood. Write for 10 minutes a day.
  • Physical Activity: Exercise has proven benefits for improving mental health. Even a 30-minute walk or stretching can boost your mood.
  • Mindfulness Meditation: Spend 5-10 minutes practicing mindfulness. Focus on your breathing and sensations without judgment.
  • Seek Support: If you continue to struggle, consider reaching out to a therapist or counselor for professional help.

Emergency Help:

If you feel you need immediate help, here are some options:

  • Emergency Services: If you're in crisis, don't hesitate to call emergency services (911) or a local mental health hotline.
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 for free, 24/7 support in the US.
  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: In the US, you can call 1-800-273-TALK (1-800-273-8255).
